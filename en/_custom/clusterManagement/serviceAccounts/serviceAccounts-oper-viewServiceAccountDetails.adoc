. On the Service Account details page, view the resource attributes of the service account in the **Properties** area.
+
--
[%header,cols="1a,4a"]
|===
|Parameter |Description

|Cluster
|The cluster to which the service account belongs.

|Project
|The project to which the service account belongs.

|Role
|The role of the service account within the project.

|Creation Time
|The time when the service account was created.

|Creator
|The user who created the service account.
|===
--

. On the **Data** tab, view the authentication information of the service account and the corresponding kubeconfig information.
+
--
* In the **Data Settings** area, click image:/images/ks-qkcp/zh/icons/eye-closed.svg[show,18,18]/image:/images/ks-qkcp/zh/icons/eye.svg[hide,18,18] in the upper right corner to show/hide the authentication information in plain text.

* In the **Kubeconfig Settings** area, click image:/images/ks-qkcp/zh/icons/copy.svg[copy,18,18] in the upper right corner to copy the kubeconfig information, and click image:/images/ks-qkcp/zh/icons/download-dark-white.svg[download,18,18] to download the **kubeconfig.yaml** file.

// Note
include::../../../../_ks_components/admonitions/note.adoc[]

The kubeconfig information contains the authentication information of the service account. You need to configure the kubeconfig information in your application to allow the application to access the KubeSphere cluster. For more information, please refer to link:https://kubernetes.io/zh/docs/concepts/configuration/organize-cluster-access-kubeconfig/[Kubernetes Official Documentation].
